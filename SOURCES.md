# Sources & References

## Scientific Research

### Project Hessdalen
- Strand, E.P. (1984-present). *Project Hessdalen*. Østfold University College, Norway. http://www.hessdalen.org
  - Primary source for scientific study of the phenomenon
  - Continuous monitoring data from 1984-present
  - Published research papers and reports

### Academic Papers

#### Peer-Reviewed Studies
- Strand, E.P. (1984). "Project Hessdalen 1984: Final Technical Report." Østfold University College.
- Teodorani, M. (2004). "A Long-Term Scientific Survey of the Hessdalen Phenomenon." *Journal of Scientific Exploration*, 18(2), 217-251.
- Hauge, B.G. (2007). "Optical Spectrum Analysis of the Hessdalen Phenomenon." Østfold University College.

#### Particle Analysis
- Monari, J., et al. (2014). "Hessdalen Lights: Chemical Analysis of Airborne Particles." Italian Committee for Project Hessdalen (ICPH).
  - Silicon, iron, and scandium particles detected
  - Correlation with light events documented

### Atmospheric Physics Research
- Various researchers (1984-present). Studies on atmospheric ionization, plasma formation, and piezoelectric effects.
- Teodorani, M. & Strand, E.P. (2001). "Hessdalen Lights: Phenomenology and Measurement Techniques."
